BABY NUTRITION REFERENCE GUIDE
==============================
Nutrient values per 100g. Source: WHO/EU infant feeding guidelines.

IRON-RICH FOODS (Critical after 6 months)
------------------------------------------
• Lentils (red, cooked): 3.3mg iron, 6mo+, low allergen, cook until mushy
• Spinach (cooked): 3.6mg iron, 6mo+, steam/wilt, mix into foods
• Beef mince (lean): 2.6mg iron, 6mo+, cook thoroughly, shred finely
• Chickpeas (cooked): 2.9mg iron, 6mo+, make hummus, remove skins
• White beans (cooked): 3.7mg iron, 6mo+, mash thoroughly
• Tofu (calcium-set): 5.4mg iron, 6mo+, medium allergen, press & cut strips
• Oats (cooked): 4.7mg iron, 6mo+, low allergen, cook until soft
• Oatmeal cereal (fortified): 25mg iron, 4mo+, critical for young infants
• Egg (cooked): 1.8mg iron, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, cook fully (no runny yolk)
• Peas (cooked): 1.5mg iron, 6mo+, MASH/FLATTEN (round shape choking hazard)

HIGH CALCIUM FOODS (Bone development)
-------------------------------------
• Cheddar cheese: 721mg calcium, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, high sodium—grate sparingly
• Tofu (calcium-set): 372mg calcium, 6mo+, medium allergen
• Ricotta cheese: 206mg calcium, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, creamy texture
• Full-fat yogurt: 121mg calcium, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, choose plain no sugar
• Spinach (cooked): 136mg calcium, 6mo+, contains oxalates (reduces absorption)
• Whole grain bread: 160mg calcium, 6mo+, medium allergen (gluten)

HIGH VITAMIN C FOODS (Boosts iron absorption—pair with iron sources)
---------------------------------------------------------------------
• Kiwi: 92.7mg vitamin C, 6mo+, medium allergen, can irritate mouth
• Strawberries: 58.8mg vitamin C, 6mo+, can trigger mouth rash
• Broccoli (steamed): 64mg vitamin C, 6mo+, steam soft, offer large florets
• Cauliflower (steamed): 48mg vitamin C, 6mo+, mild flavor
• Butternut squash: 21mg vitamin C, 6mo+, excellent first food
• Mango (ripe): 36.4mg vitamin C, 6mo+, slippery—mash for young babies
• Zucchini (cooked): 12.9mg vitamin C, 6mo+, mild, well-tolerated
• Peas (cooked): 14.2mg vitamin C, 6mo+, MASH/FLATTEN (choking hazard)

HIGH PROTEIN FOODS (Growth & development)
------------------------------------------
• Chicken breast: 31g protein, 6mo+, poach until 75°C, shred finely
• Turkey mince: 27g protein, 6mo+, cook thoroughly
• Beef mince: 26g protein, 6mo+, excellent iron + protein combo
• Salmon: 25g protein, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, check bones, limit 2x/week (mercury)
• Cheddar cheese: 25g protein, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, high sodium
• Cod: 18g protein, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, low mercury white fish
• Tofu: 15g protein, 6mo+, medium allergen
• Egg: 13g protein, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, fully cook
• Oats: 13.2g protein, 6mo+, higher protein than rice cereal
• Full-fat yogurt: 3.5g protein, 6mo+, HIGH ALLERGEN, probiotics

EXCELLENT FIRST FOODS (6 months)
---------------------------------
• Sweet potato: vitamin A, naturally sweet, bake/steam until soft
• Avocado: healthy fats for brain, mash ripe flesh
• Banana (ripe): easy to digest, mash with brown spots
• Butternut squash: vitamin A, roast/steam, mash or spears
• Pumpkin: beta-carotene, naturally sweet
• Pear (ripe): gentle on digestion, helps constipation
• Carrot (cooked): vitamin A, ALWAYS COOK until soft (raw = choking hazard)
• Zucchini: mild, adds moisture to meals
• Oatmeal: iron-fortified, cook in milk/water

ALLERGEN RISK SUMMARY
---------------------
HIGH (introduce early in small amounts, one at a time):
- Egg, Dairy (yogurt, cheese, butter), Fish (salmon, cod), Wheat/gluten

MEDIUM (introduce with caution):
- Kiwi, Tofu/soy, Barley (gluten)

LOW/NONE:
- Most fruits, vegetables, oats, rice, lentils, meats

CRITICAL SAFETY RULES
---------------------
• NEVER serve raw egg or runny yolk (salmonella)
• NEVER serve whole grapes, cherry tomatoes, whole nuts (choking)
• ALWAYS cook carrots until soft for under 2 years
• ALWAYS mash/flatten peas (round = choking hazard)
• ALWAYS check fish for bones
• ALWAYS cook meat thoroughly until no pink remains
• Honey: NEVER for under 12 months (botulism)—handled by safety layer

AGE APPROPRIATENESS
-------------------
• 4-6 months: Iron-fortified cereals, smooth purees, single ingredients
• 6-9 months: Mashed textures, soft strips for BLW, introduce allergens
• 9-12 months: Finger foods, soft pieces, pincer grasp foods
• 12-18 months: Family food adapted, continue avoiding whole nuts/hard foods
• 18-24 months: Near-adult foods, reduced sodium focus

FOOD PAIRING TIPS
-----------------
• Pair iron + vitamin C foods to maximize absorption
• Example: Lentils + tomatoes, Beef + broccoli, Beans + mango
• Add small amount of fat (olive oil, avocado) to vegetable purees for vitamin absorption
